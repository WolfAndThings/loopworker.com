The Problem

Your renderings take too long, cost too much, and still don't sell the lifestyle.

Traditional renderings are painfully expensive

A 3D rendering firm charges $5,000-15,000 per project. A full marketing package with multiple angles, interior and exterior, day and night — you're looking at $20-40K. And if the design changes after the renders are done? Start over. Revisions are billed by the hour at rates that make your architect look cheap.

Staging is expensive and limited

Physical staging costs $2,000-5,000 per room. Virtual staging from traditional firms runs $100-300 per room and still looks like a rendering from 2018 — furniture floating above floors, lighting that doesn't match the windows, shadows going the wrong direction. Buyers can tell. It undermines trust at exactly the moment you need it most.

Marketing materials take weeks

You need visuals for the brochure, the website, the social campaign, the investor deck, and the sales office — and you need them yesterday. Traditional rendering pipelines take 4-8 weeks from brief to delivery. By the time your marketing materials are ready, your competitor has already started pre-sales on the project down the street.

You're selling square footage, not a life

Floor plans and spec sheets don't sell properties. People don't buy a 2BR/2BA with 1,200 square feet. They buy the morning coffee on that balcony, the dinner party in that kitchen, the view from that bedroom at sunset. If your marketing doesn't sell the feeling, you're competing on price — and that's a race to the bottom.
