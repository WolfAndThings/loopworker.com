The Problem

You know your food is good. Your Instagram doesn't.

Photography is expensive and infrequent

A professional food photographer charges $3,000-5,000 per session. So you book one, maybe twice a year. Your fall menu gets shot in January. Your specials never get photographed at all. And when you finally get the images back two weeks later, half of them feel generic.

Your Instagram is a graveyard

You posted three times last month. Two of them were reposts from customers. The other was a blurry shot your server took on an iPhone 11. Meanwhile, the taco spot down the street posts daily and has three times your following. You know consistency matters — you just don't have the content to sustain it.

Your menu looks outdated

The photos on your website are from 2022. Your DoorDash listing uses the same five images for forty items. Customers are making decisions based on visuals that don't represent what you're actually serving. Every unappetizing photo is a lost order.

Your competitors look better than you

The places winning on social aren't necessarily better restaurants. They just have better content. Better lighting, better angles, better consistency. When someone is scrolling for somewhere to eat on Friday night, they choose the place that looks the part. Perception is everything.
